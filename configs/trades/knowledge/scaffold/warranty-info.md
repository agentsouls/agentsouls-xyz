## How to Make a Warranty Claim

1. **Call {{COMPANY_NAME}} at {{COMPANY_PHONE}}**
2. We'll schedule a technician to diagnose the issue
3. If the issue is covered under warranty:
   - **Parts:** Manufacturer covers the cost of the replacement part
   - **Labor:** Covered under {{COMPANY_NAME}}'s labor warranty if within the labor warranty period. After the labor warranty expires, labor charges apply even if the part is under manufacturer warranty.
4. The technician will handle the warranty claim paperwork

**Agent script:** "If your equipment is still under manufacturer warranty, the replacement parts would be covered. There may be a labor charge depending on when the system was installed. Our technician can check the warranty status on-site and go over everything before doing any work."

## FAQ

**Q: My system is 8 years old. Is it still under warranty?**
A: Most manufacturer parts warranties are 10 years if the system was registered. Our technician can look up the warranty status using the serial number when they come out.

**Q: Do I need proof of maintenance for my warranty?**
A: Some manufacturers require proof of annual maintenance for warranty claims. Our maintenance plan members have maintenance records on file automatically.

**Q: The part is under warranty but you're charging for labor?**
A: Manufacturer warranties cover the cost of the replacement part. Labor to install it is covered under our labor warranty for {{INSTALL_WARRANTY_PERIOD}} after the original installation. After that period, labor charges apply at our standard rates (with a {{DISCOUNT_PERCENT}}% discount for maintenance plan members).
