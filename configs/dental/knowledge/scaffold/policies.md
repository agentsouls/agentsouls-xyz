  nearest emergency room.]

### What Qualifies as a Dental Emergency?

- Severe, uncontrollable pain
- Knocked-out (avulsed) tooth — time-sensitive, see a dentist within 30 minutes
- Broken or cracked tooth with sharp edges or pain
- Significant swelling of the face, jaw, or gums
- Uncontrolled bleeding from the mouth
- Abscess or pus drainage
- Injury to the jaw

### Not an Emergency (But Should Be Seen Soon)

- Lost filling or crown (no pain)
- Chipped tooth (no sharp edges, no pain)
- Minor toothache that responds to over-the-counter pain relief
- Broken orthodontic wire or bracket
