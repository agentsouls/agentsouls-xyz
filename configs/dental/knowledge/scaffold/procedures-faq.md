## Cleanings & Preventive Care

### How often should I get a cleaning?
Most patients benefit from a professional cleaning and exam every six months.
Some patients with gum disease or other risk factors may be recommended for
cleanings every three to four months (periodontal maintenance). Your dentist
and hygienist will recommend the best schedule for you.

### What's the difference between a regular cleaning and a deep cleaning?
A regular cleaning (prophylaxis) removes plaque and tartar from above and
slightly below the gum line. It's a preventive treatment for healthy gums.

A deep cleaning (scaling and root planing) goes further below the gum line to
remove tartar buildup on the root surfaces and smooth them to help the gums
reattach. It's a therapeutic treatment for patients with gum disease. Local
anesthesia is typically used for comfort.

### Are dental X-rays safe?
Yes. Modern digital X-rays use very low levels of radiation — significantly
less than older film-based X-rays. A set of dental X-rays exposes you to less
radiation than you receive from everyday environmental sources in a single day.
We follow the ALARA principle (as low as reasonably achievable) and only take
X-rays when clinically necessary.

### Do I really need X-rays if my teeth feel fine?
X-rays allow us to see issues that aren't visible during a visual exam —
cavities between teeth, bone loss, infections at the root tips, impacted teeth,
and other problems that may not cause symptoms until they become more serious.
Early detection through X-rays can save time, money, and discomfort.

## Fillings

### What are fillings made of?
We use tooth-colored composite resin fillings that match the natural appearance
of your teeth. [We do not place amalgam (silver) fillings.] Composite fillings
bond directly to the tooth structure, which often allows us to preserve more of
your natural tooth.

### Do fillings hurt?
We use local anesthesia to completely numb the area before placing a filling.
You should not feel pain during the procedure. After the numbness wears off,
some mild sensitivity is normal for a few days — this typically resolves on
its own.

### How long do fillings last?
Composite fillings generally last [5-10] years, depending on the size and
location of the filling, your bite, and oral hygiene habits. We monitor your
fillings at each checkup and will let you know if any need to be replaced.

## Crowns

### What is a dental crown?
A crown (sometimes called a "cap") is a custom-made restoration that covers
the entire visible portion of a tooth. It restores the tooth's shape, size,
strength, and appearance. Crowns are recommended for teeth that are cracked,
severely decayed, weakened by a large filling, or have had a root canal.

### How many appointments does a crown require?
[Traditional crowns require two appointments: one to prepare the tooth and take
impressions, and a second appointment (about two weeks later) to place the
permanent crown. A temporary crown protects the tooth between visits.]

[With our CEREC technology, we can create and place your crown in a single
visit — no temporary crown needed.]

### Does getting a crown hurt?
The tooth is completely numbed with local anesthesia during the preparation
appointment. You should not feel pain during the procedure. Some mild
sensitivity and soreness are normal for a few days after the permanent crown
is placed.

### How long do crowns last?
With proper care, dental crowns typically last [10-15+] years. Porcelain and
zirconia crowns are very durable. Good oral hygiene, regular checkups, and
avoiding habits like grinding or chewing ice help extend the life of your crown.

## Root Canals

### What is a root canal?
A root canal is a treatment to save a tooth that has an infected or damaged
nerve (pulp). The dentist removes the infected tissue from inside the tooth,
cleans and shapes the canals, and seals them. The tooth is then typically
restored with a crown for strength.

### Do root canals hurt?
Modern root canals are much more comfortable than their reputation suggests.
We use effective local anesthesia and [sedation options if needed]. Most
patients compare the experience to getting a filling. The goal of a root canal
is actually to relieve the pain caused by the infection — most patients feel
significantly better after the procedure.

### How long does a root canal take?
A root canal typically takes [60-90] minutes in a single appointment. Some
complex cases (back teeth with multiple canals) may require a second visit.

### Will I need a crown after a root canal?
In most cases, yes. After a root canal, the tooth becomes more brittle over
time. A crown protects the tooth from fracturing and restores full function.
[We typically place the crown [2-4 weeks] after the root canal.]

### What if I don't get a root canal?
An infected tooth will not heal on its own. Without treatment, the infection
can spread to the surrounding bone and tissue, potentially forming an abscess.
The alternatives are extraction (removing the tooth) or doing nothing, which
we do not recommend as the infection will worsen.

## Teeth Whitening

### How white will my teeth get?
Professional whitening can brighten your teeth by [several shades / 3-8 shades],
depending on the starting color, the type of staining, and the whitening method
used. Results vary by individual. Your dentist can give you a realistic
expectation during a consultation.

### Is whitening safe for my teeth?
Yes, when performed professionally or with dentist-supervised products.
Professional whitening products are formulated to minimize enamel damage and
sensitivity. Over-the-counter products and at-home "hacks" can be more
unpredictable.

### Will whitening make my teeth sensitive?
Temporary sensitivity is the most common side effect. It typically lasts
[24-72 hours] after treatment and resolves on its own. [We offer desensitizing
treatments and sensitivity-reducing toothpaste to help.]

### How long do whitening results last?
Results typically last [6 months to 2 years], depending on your diet and
habits. Coffee, tea, red wine, and tobacco can stain teeth more quickly.
Periodic touch-ups with take-home trays help maintain results.

### Can I whiten my crowns or veneers?
No. Whitening products only work on natural tooth enamel. Crowns, veneers,
and bonding do not change color with whitening. If you're planning cosmetic
work, it's best to whiten first so restorations can be matched to your
brighter shade.

## Dental Implants

### What is a dental implant?
A dental implant is a permanent replacement for a missing tooth. It consists
of three parts: a titanium post (surgically placed in the jawbone), an
abutment (connector), and a custom crown on top. The result looks, feels, and
functions like a natural tooth.

### Am I a candidate for implants?
Most adults with adequate jawbone density and good overall health are candidates.
A consultation with [imaging/CBCT scan] is needed to evaluate your bone and plan
placement. [Bone grafting is available if bone density is insufficient.]

### How long does the implant process take?
The full process typically takes [3-6 months] from start to finish:
1. Consultation and planning: [1-2 visits]
2. Implant placement (surgery): [1 visit, 1-2 hours]
3. Healing period (osseointegration): [3-4 months]
4. Abutment and crown placement: [1-2 visits]

### Does implant surgery hurt?
The surgery is performed under local anesthesia [and sedation if desired].
Most patients report that the procedure is less uncomfortable than they
expected. Post-operative discomfort is typically manageable with over-the-counter
pain medication and resolves within [3-7 days].

### How long do implants last?
With proper care and regular dental visits, dental implants can last a lifetime.
The crown on top may need replacement after [10-15] years due to normal wear.

### Are implants covered by insurance?
Coverage varies by plan. [Some dental plans cover a portion of implant costs.
Medical insurance may cover implants if tooth loss was due to an accident or
medical condition.] We can provide a pre-treatment estimate to submit to your
insurance.

## X-Rays (Additional Questions)

### How often do I need X-rays?
Frequency depends on your oral health, age, risk for disease, and any symptoms.
General guidelines:
- **New patients:** Full-mouth or panoramic X-rays at the first visit.
- **Recall patients:** Bitewing X-rays [every 12-24 months] to check for
  cavities between teeth.
- **Panoramic X-rays:** [Every 3-5 years] or as needed for treatment planning.

### Can I decline X-rays?
You have the right to decline any procedure. However, without X-rays, the
dentist's ability to diagnose certain conditions is limited. We will always
explain why X-rays are recommended and respect your decision.

## General

### I'm nervous about going to the dentist. Can you help?
Absolutely. Dental anxiety is very common, and we take it seriously. We offer:
- [Nitrous oxide (laughing gas) for relaxation]
- [Oral sedation for moderate anxiety]
- [IV sedation for severe anxiety or longer procedures]
- [A caring, patient team that goes at your pace]
- [Headphones, blankets, and other comfort amenities]
Let us know about your concerns when scheduling, and we'll make sure your
visit is as comfortable as possible.

### What should I do if a tooth gets knocked out?
This is a time-sensitive emergency:
1. Pick up the tooth by the crown (top part), not the root.
2. If dirty, gently rinse with milk or saline (do not scrub).
3. Try to place the tooth back in the socket. If not possible, keep it moist
   in milk, saline, or between your cheek and gum.
4. Call us immediately at [Practice Phone] or go to the nearest emergency room.
5. The best chance of saving the tooth is within 30 minutes of the injury.
