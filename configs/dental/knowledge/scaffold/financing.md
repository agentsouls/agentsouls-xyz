## HSA & FSA Accounts

Dental care is an eligible expense for **Health Savings Accounts (HSA)** and
**Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)**. You can use your HSA/FSA debit card to
pay for dental services at our office.

### Tips for HSA/FSA
- **FSA funds expire** at the end of the plan year (or grace period). Don't
  lose your benefits — schedule treatment before your deadline.
- **HSA funds roll over** year to year, so you can save for larger treatments.
- Most preventive dental care (cleanings, exams, X-rays) is 100% eligible.
- Cosmetic-only procedures (whitening) are generally NOT eligible.

## Insurance Maximization

If you have dental insurance, our team can help you make the most of your
benefits:

1. **Use your preventive benefits.** Most plans cover two cleanings and exams
   per year at 100%. Don't leave these on the table.
2. **Plan treatment around your benefit year.** If you need extensive work,
   we can split treatment across two benefit years to maximize coverage.
3. **Pre-authorization.** For major work, we submit pre-authorization to your
   insurance so you know your coverage before starting treatment.
4. **Dual coverage.** If you have two dental plans (e.g., through your employer
   and your spouse's), we coordinate benefits to reduce your costs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do you require payment in full at the time of service?**
A: For insurance patients, we collect the estimated patient portion at the
time of service. For uninsured patients, payment in full is expected unless a
payment plan has been arranged in advance.

**Q: What if I can't afford the recommended treatment?**
A: Talk to us. We can discuss phased treatment plans (prioritizing the most
urgent needs first), financing options, or alternative treatment approaches.
We want to help you get the care you need.

**Q: Is there a discount for paying cash?**
A: [Yes, we offer a [X]% courtesy discount for uninsured patients who pay in
full at the time of service. / We offer competitive self-pay rates. Ask us
for a quote.]

**Q: Can I use multiple financing options together?**
A: In some cases, yes. For example, you could use your FSA for part of the
cost and CareCredit for the remainder. Talk to our financial coordinator for
help structuring your payment.
