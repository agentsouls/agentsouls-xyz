## Preventive Care

### Comprehensive Oral Exam
- **Description:** A thorough evaluation of your teeth, gums, jaw, and oral tissues. The dentist checks for cavities, gum disease, oral cancer, bite alignment, and any other concerns.
- **Duration:** [45-60] minutes
- **Frequency:** Recommended [every 6 months / annually]
- **Who it's for:** All patients. Required as a first visit for new patients.
- **What to expect:** The dentist will examine your mouth, may take X-rays, and discuss any findings with you.

### Dental Cleaning (Prophylaxis)
- **Description:** A professional cleaning to remove plaque, tartar, and surface stains. Includes polishing and flossing.
- **Duration:** [30-45] minutes
- **Frequency:** [Every 6 months] for most patients.
- **Who it's for:** Patients with generally healthy gums.
- **What to expect:** A hygienist will use specialized instruments to clean above and below the gum line, then polish your teeth.

### Deep Cleaning (Scaling & Root Planing)
- **Description:** A deeper cleaning for patients with gum disease. Removes tartar buildup below the gum line and smooths root surfaces to help gums reattach.
- **Duration:** [60-90] minutes per quadrant
- **Frequency:** As recommended by the dentist based on gum health.
- **Who it's for:** Patients diagnosed with periodontal (gum) disease.
- **What to expect:** Local anesthesia is typically used. May require [2-4] appointments depending on severity.

### Dental X-Rays
- **Description:** Digital imaging to detect issues not visible during a visual exam, including cavities between teeth, bone loss, impacted teeth, and infections.
- **Duration:** [10-15] minutes
- **Types offered:** Bitewing, periapical, panoramic, [CBCT/cone beam]
- **Safety:** Modern digital X-rays use very low radiation — significantly less than older film X-rays.

### Fluoride Treatment
- **Description:** A concentrated fluoride application to strengthen enamel and help prevent cavities.
- **Duration:** [5-10] minutes (applied after cleaning)
- **Who it's for:** [Children and adults at higher risk for cavities]

### Dental Sealants
- **Description:** A thin protective coating applied to the chewing surfaces of back teeth (molars) to prevent cavities.
- **Duration:** [15-20] minutes
- **Who it's for:** [Children and teens, typically ages 6-14]
