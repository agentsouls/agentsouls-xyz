## Insurance Verification Process

### Before Your Appointment

1. **Provide your insurance information** when scheduling — we need:
   - Insurance company name
   - Member/subscriber ID number
   - Group number (if applicable)
   - Subscriber name and date of birth
2. **We verify your benefits** before your appointment. Our team contacts your
   insurance company to confirm:
   - Plan eligibility and effective dates
   - Annual maximum benefit
   - Remaining benefit balance
   - Deductible amount and status
   - Coverage percentages by category (preventive, basic, major)
   - Waiting periods or exclusions
3. **We provide an estimate** of your expected out-of-pocket cost. This is an
   estimate only — final coverage is determined by your insurance company after
   the claim is processed.

### Important Notes

- **Insurance verification is not a guarantee of payment.** Your insurance
  company makes the final determination on coverage and payment.
- **Pre-authorization** may be required for certain procedures (crowns, root
  canals, implants, orthodontics). We handle the pre-authorization process.
- **Waiting periods** — Some plans have waiting periods for major procedures.
  We can help you understand if this applies to your plan.
- **Annual maximums** — Most dental plans have an annual maximum benefit
  (typically $[1,000-2,500] per year). Once you reach this maximum, additional
  costs are your responsibility until the plan year resets.
- **Plan year** — Most dental plans reset on [January 1 / your employer's
  plan year]. Unused benefits do not roll over.

## Common Insurance Questions

**Q: Do I need a referral to see a specialist?**
A: [Most PPO plans do not require referrals. HMO plans typically do. Check with
your insurance company or ask us.]

**Q: How often does insurance cover cleanings?**
A: [Most plans cover two preventive visits (cleanings + exams) per year at
100%. Some plans cover them every 6 months from the date of your last cleaning;
others follow a calendar year.]

**Q: Does insurance cover cosmetic procedures?**
A: [Generally, no. Cosmetic procedures like whitening and veneers are typically
not covered. However, some restorative procedures that also improve appearance
(crowns, bonding) may have partial coverage.]

**Q: What if I have two insurance plans?**
A: [We can coordinate benefits between two plans (dual coverage). This may
reduce your out-of-pocket costs. Please provide both insurance cards when
scheduling.]

**Q: My insurance was denied — what now?**
A: [Our team can help you understand the denial reason and, if appropriate,
file an appeal. Contact us at [Practice Phone] to discuss your specific
situation.]
